What is a Mountain Bike?

A mountain bike refers to any bike that is designed to be ridden off-road. Here at Summit Bike Co we sell two kinds of mountain bikes: Hardtail mountain bikes are called hardtail because they have a suspension fork at the front and Full Suspension mountain bikes which have a front suspension fork and a rear shock absorber, and are the most accomplished across rough terrain. The mountain bike best suited to you depends on the kind of riding you want to do and, of course, fits into your budget. You can get expert advice on these at any of our Summit Bike Co stores.

What are mountain bike frames made from?

Mountain bike frames are usually made from steel, aluminium or carbon fibre. Wheels are strong with thick tyres and deep treads. The best mountain bike will be one that suits the kind of riding you want to do, at the right price.

What does the 'amount of suspension travel' mean in mountain biking?

Fortunately, bike manufacturers categorise bikes according to what terrain they think is most suitable for the bike they've designed. This roughly equates to the amount of suspension travel the bike has. Less travel means that the bike's weight is reduced — shorter travel shocks with lighter chassis all keep the weight down. More travel means the overall weight of the bike increases, but the suspension's design ensures the effort you put through your pedals still gets efficiently transferred to the rear wheel.

What is a Rear Derailleur?

A rear derailleur (also known as a 'rear mech') is the mechanism that moves the bike chain from cog-to-cog on the cassette (a cluster of sprockets that are a part of the drivetrain for the bike chain) at the rear wheel of the bike. The right-hand gear shifter on the handlebars pulls a cable (or uses electronic signals) to move the derailleur, which in-turn moves the chain onto the appropriate cog on the cassette. The rear derailleur and the cassette work together to ensure a smooth shifting between gears. There are many different models but they all share the same basic design and functionality.

What are Hardtail Mountain Bikes?

A hardtail mountain bike uses a suspension fork at the front of the bike that gives cushioning against uneven terrain, along with improved grip and handling. As hardtails don't possess rear suspension, this makes them more cost effective, easier to maintain and lighter in wieght than the more complicated full suspension bikes - making them a good choice for beginners.

Hardtail Mountain Bike Wheel Sizes

Most brands use either 27.5” (also known as 650b) or 29” wheels. 29” mountain bike wheels roll faster and so have a speed advantage, but on very tight, technical terrain they do not handle as well as the 27.5” wheels. Which is best hardtail mountain bike for you? This guide will help you pick the best style of hardtail for your requirements.

Cross Country Hardtails

Cross-country hardtails are designed for riding over large distances, whether in a race or riding with your friends, and are sometimes equipped with a fork lockout to make them even more effective on smooth ground. Most cross-country hardtails have light aluminium frames, making them more affordable. The more expensive models comprise carbon fibre or steel to further reduce weight.

Trail Hardtails

Trail hardtail mountain bikes (otherwise known as 'hardcore' MTBs) are still capable of covering big distances but are great when the trail starts to point downhill. This makes them a popular choice for new riders. Trail hardtails use suspension forks which have longer and more control over uneven terrain. Compared to cross-country hard tails, they also have slightly heavier but tougher frames and equipment, which makes them capable on demanding, rocky trails. These bikes are also available in 27.5” (650b) and 29”.

Full Suspension Mountain Bikes vs Hardtail Bikes

A full suspension mountain bike allows for a more comfortable and controlled ride on off-road trails than you'd get from a hardtail mountain bike. Full Suspension bikes have a suspension fork at the front of the bike and a shock absorber at the rear, giving a smoother ride as the wheels can move up and over bumps, lumps, roots and rocks opening up the opportunity to explore the more challenging terrains and obstacles. What's the best full suspension mountain bike for you? To work that out, decide what type of terrain you want to tackle and the amount of travel they have on offer.

Trail Full Suspension

Trail bikes will let you tackle rougher terrain with greater control and stability, and at higher speeds.

Enduro Full Suspension

Part of the Trail bike family, if you're looking to take on particularly challenging terrain once in a while, Enduro bikes (also known as All Mountain bikes) are the best full suspension mountain bikes for you. To help increase stability, they are slightly longer than trail and cross-country bikes, and will use wider bars and shorter stems for more reactive steering. Enduro bikes are designed to tackle the toughest tracks and bike park laps while still being light enough to pedal to the top of the hills, so combining light weight with good travel capability.

Cross Country Full Suspension

Lightweight, Cross Country full suspension mountain bikes will normally boast around 100mm (3.9in) of suspension travel and the lightest use carbon fibre frames. The intention is to add some comfort and increase traction at the rear wheel, all without hampering pedalling efficiency and keeping a low weight. Cross-country mountain bikes are designed to be ridden uphill just as fast as they are downhill.
