Mountain Bike Sizing Guide

What Size Mountain Bike do I need?

The below chart should shows you the correct size of mountain bike based on your height. Measure your height accurately and check the manufacturer's measurements too. You can also visit us in store to speak to an experienced member of staff. All Summit Bike Co mountain bikes are available in XS (extra small), S (small), M (medium), L (large), or XL (extra large).

Height (centimetres) Suggested Frame Size
148cm - 158cm 13" - 14" (XS)
158cm - 168cm 15" - 16" (S)
168cm - 178cm 17" - 18" (M)
178cm - 185cm 19" - 20" (L)
185cm - 193cm 21" - 22" (XL)

Women's mountain bikes tend to be shorter in length and have narrower handlebars. They also have specific saddles and often have their own colour scheme too. Like other mountain bikes, these designs are measured in the same way (from the centre of the bottom bracket to the top of the seat tube) but there's no reason why you shouldn't try a mens or unisex mountain bike.

How are mountain bikes measured?

Mountain bikes are measured from the centre of the bottom bracket (BB) to the top of the seat tube. To give a rider more stand over height, mountain bikes top tubes often slope down and join the seat tube lower than road bikes. So it's worth taking your inside leg measurement and comparing it to the manufacturers model. Always check the manufacturer's measurements or visit a store to speak to an experienced member of staff.
